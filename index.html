Cycle 01

The first 90 days

Build the habit of proof, transfer, and visible decisions before expanding the program.

Days 01–15

Establish the mandate

Make the Labs real

  • Confirm sponsor and steering group.
  • Publish the charter and selection rubric.
  • Announce the first theme and intake window.
  • Schedule AI Day, demo, and decision forums.

Exit: Sponsor approval and a published intake.

Days 16–30

Build the first portfolio

Select for adoption, not novelty

  • Run AI Day and problem-refinement office hours.
  • Capture Accessibility and UBE baselines.
  • Select no more than three projects.
  • Name pilot users, owners, and risk paths.

Exit: Every project has a sponsor, baseline, and consulted owner.

Days 31–75

Incubate with real users

Turn evidence into a decision

  • Run weekly pilot reviews and biweekly unblock sessions.
  • Give Fellows a delivery or adoption responsibility.
  • Begin reviews early; measure continuously.
  • Start the transfer kit as soon as adoption is credible.

Exit: A real-user result and a recommended decision.

Days 76–90

Decide in public

Make useful work travel

  • Demo outcome and adoption evidence—not model novelty.
  • Scale, transfer, extend once, or sunset every project.
  • Record ownership, support bounds, and next-cycle learnings.
  • Recognize Fellows and partner teams.

Exit: A documented outcome for every project.

Operating playbook

Small rules that protect the signal.

Use the Labs to turn local expertise into company-wide leverage—without becoming a shadow platform team.

01

Start with a problem

Measure a painful current workflow. The AI mechanism is secondary to the result.

02

Name the next owner early

A prototype has no path to selection without a team that could operate it.

03

Design for transfer

Package architecture, evaluation, cost, safety, onboarding, and a bounded support model.

04

Credit the system

Partner visibly with central AI and platform teams. The brand is connective leadership.

Executive decision demo

Six things to show

  1. User problem and company relevance
  2. Baseline versus pilot result
  3. Short demo or user evidence
  4. Adoption evidence and receiving owner
  5. Risk and operating-cost status
  6. A precise requested decision
